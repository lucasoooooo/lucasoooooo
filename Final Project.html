Data Visualization¶

Next, it is important that the data can be visualized, and one way to do this is with graphs. In the bottom graph, I have created a graph showing the frequency of crashes that happend at different events, separated if there was another vehicle involved or not.

#tallies number of accidents that happend at each type of movement to see what is most frequent
motor_other_vehicle = motorcycles[motorcycles['HARM_EVENT_DESC'] == "Other Vehicle"]
motor_own_fault = motorcycles[motorcycles['HARM_EVENT_DESC'] != "Other Vehicle"]

#create Series of Motorcylists faults and other vehicles faults
movement_t_ov = motor_other_vehicle['MOVEMENT_DESC'].value_counts()
movement_t_of = motor_own_fault['MOVEMENT_DESC'].value_counts()

#Create Conjoin series
movement_types_motor_cat = pd.concat([movement_t_ov,movement_t_of], axis=1)
movement_types_motor_cat.columns = ["Other Vehicle", "Own Fault"]

movement_types_motorcycles_bar = movement_types_motor_cat.plot.bar(xlabel = "Movement Type",
                                                                   ylabel = "Total Accidents",
                                                                   stacked=True,
                                                                   title = "Total Motorcycle Crashes based on Movement Type From 2015-2020")
